# Escalation Paths

_Last updated: 2026-05-12 | Status: DONE_

## When to escalate
Escalate when you're blocked, when a decision is outside your scope, or when something affects the team and you're unsure how to handle it.

Don't wait 3 days to say you're stuck. Escalate early, not late.

## Escalation tiers

### Tier 1: Peer / Team Lead
- Blocked on a task → ask your buddy or the relevant domain owner
- Unclear requirements → ask the person who filed the issue or the PM
- Code review not happening → ping the reviewer directly in Slack

### Tier 2: Engineering Manager (Lukas)
Escalate to Lukas when:
- You're blocked for more than half a day and Tier 1 didn't resolve it
- You're being asked to do something that feels outside scope or ethically wrong
- There's a team dynamics issue you don't feel comfortable raising with a peer
- A customer-facing incident is P0 or P1 and needs coordination

### Tier 3: VP of Engineering
Lukas escalates to the VP when:
- An incident threatens SLA commitments to enterprise customers
- A hire, firing, or PIP decision needs VP sign-off
- Budget decisions above Lukas's approval threshold
- Cross-team dependencies that can't be resolved at EM level

## Triggers are real
These aren't hypothetical — if you hit these situations, escalate. The table exists so nobody has to guess.
